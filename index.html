Drawing App

michellescolorpicker.surge.sh

For the drawing app, I partnered with Ian Zambrano to create multiple versions of our app to include the ability to draw, create different shapes, asterisks, flowers, and other tools. Our goal was to make a functioning drawing app that effectively created shapes where size was adjustable. We faced many problems with how to center the shapes, so once drawn, they were perfectly centered where the mouse originally was. I learned that Snap Berkeley has a variety of libraries that can be downloaded and used to create animations in our coding app, provide a variety of colors to draw with, and create menus to choose different tools. Although this is a work in progress, we will continue to make new versions to improve our drawing app.

Scratch Game

michellesscratchgame.surge.sh

I based my Scratch Game on a cat cafe where you control the cat paw to collect the falling boba. My first idea included coding an entire cafe game where the player was able to make different drinks for customers. However, with many complications and my minimal knowledge on coding, I decided to make my game more simple. I was inspired by a YouTube video by fluffkeys who had a similar cafe style game. To make it my own, I made it into a cat boba cafe where the player is the cat paw who catches falling boba for their matcha boba drink. I ended up learning how to add different components to my game to make it more enticing such as background music that changed from the start to the end, different backgrounds with a congratulating ending scene, and the ability for your score to be tracked based on how much boba the paw collected. This all contributed to my preliminary knowledge of coding and acted as a formal introduction to it.

AP Research Paper

michellesapresearch.surge.sh

This is a project that lasted an entire school year where I worked on deciding a research topic, planning a procedure on how to collect my data, then executing my research by interviewing around 10 students that aligned with my targeted population. In the end, I encountered the most challenges with forming my research procedures and finding the most efficient way to collect my data. I ended up using both quantitative and qualitative means of data collection that was then combined to support one another in my conclusion. There were scheduling conflicts between the interviews and I also had to create questions that were unique to each student, but in the end I learned how to manage my time, compile data into a research paper, and work on my public speaking skills since our final was a thorough presentation on our research. This class acted as a base that helped improve my writing skills and my ability to formulate instantaneous responses.
